USNCO Course

Course Description

An accelerated USNCO prep course, taught by a USNCO finalist and designed for students serious on competing for the exam. This course will prepare students for the USA National Chemistry Olympiad (USNCO) with weekly lectures and practice problems based on past competition exams.

About Instructor

Junyu Yang (Jun)

Jun is currently a PhD candidate at Harvard Chemistry and Chemical Biology. Previously, she graduated with dual Bachelor of Science degrees in Chemistry and Chemical Engineering from MIT. Through her several years of research experience in inorganic chemistry, she has accrued five publications in reputable journals.

In high school, she was a two-time Chemistry Olympiad Top 20 Finalist. Additionally, as team captain, she led Troy High School’s Science Bowl team to Nationals for the first time in over a decade. She also competed as part of Troy High School’s Science Olympiad team, where she won medals in Chemistry Lab, Protein Modeling, and Dynamic Planet at Nationals and other nationally-competitive tournaments. Later, Jun would become an Events Director at the MIT Science Olympiad Invitational and an event supervisor at tournaments throughout the nation. In her spare time, Jun teaches students chemistry-related topics such as AP Chemistry, organic chemistry, and Chemistry Olympiad.

Course Structure


  • The course will run from October 10th to March 15th

  • 1 hour lecture, 1 hour problem solving. Later we will add an extra 1 hour for advanced topics

  • Designed for advanced topics on the USNCO @Wed (7-9ET)

  • Old USNCO exams assigned as HW. End-of-chapter problems recommended and reviewed

  • Enrollment capped at 20 so students get individual attention

  • All lectures will be recorded!
